Master Thesis (2020 - ...)

Hacettepe University

My master thesis title is : Estimation of Seismic Wave Propagation Patterns on Different Soil Types Using Neural Network Models
For master thesis, I downloaded 1.5 million earthquake records from Japanese Kik-Net database. In Kik-Net system there are two seismographs at every point, one is at the level of bedrock and the other at surface. The difference of accelerograms of these two seismographs, should contain the information of soil column between them. In my thesis, I am using neural networks to extract patterns of seismic wave propagation on different soil types.
My thesis requires processing of 1.5 million earthquake recordings. In addition to that I had to visualize the data in different ways for a better understanding of the data.
Before deciding to work on this subject I made a project (much simpler version of my thesis) for my Neural Network Class (CMP684). In this project I used Turkey's seismograph data and earthquake recordings to determine soil type (Soft Soil, Hard Soil, Rock etc.) of the seismograph site.
You can view this project on aliakgoz/ai_vs30_prediction.

Kik-Net Earthquake Data Visualization
As stated above, I processed 1.5 million earthquake recordings for my thesis. And I made a data visualization tool for myself, to check some properties of data and to get more insight. There are six accelerograms recorded for every earthquake at Kik-Net.
  • EW1: Recorded by seismograph at bedrock level, which works on East West axis
  • EW2: Recorded by seismograph at surface level, which works on East West axis
  • NS1: Recorded by seismograph at bedrock level, which works on North South axis
  • NS2: Recorded by seismograph at surface level, which works on North South axis
  • UD1: Recorded by seismograph at bedrock level, which works on Up Down axis
  • UD2: Recorded by seismograph at surface level, which works on Up Down axis
For standardization purposes, I calculated (Fast-Fourier Transformation) the frequency domain spectra from these accelerograms. My theory is EW2 recording (surface level) is a result of interaction between EW1 (bedrock level) and Soil Column. Hence, I am using EW1 and soil column values as input to the neural network models and EW2 as the output.
Soil column values consists of two values (Vs and Vp) for each depth value from surface to the bedrock level.
  • Vs (m/s): Shear Wave Velocity of soil
  • Vp (m/s): Pressure Wave Velocity of soil
and at the bottom soil column Vs-Vp values of the station given.
